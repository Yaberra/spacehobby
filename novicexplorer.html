Novice Explorers

I recall my first conversation with my daughter one afternoon. She looked up from her phone, ‘Mom I want to be an Astro Biologist.”

“What is an Astro Biologist?,” I asked. “It’s the study of life forms on other planets and space,” she said matter of fact. I was intrigued by the the specifity of her interest, curiosity and depth of exploration on the subject.

I learnt a lot from her and from my own effort to under the topic a bit better. Already, we trade stories and articles to see what crosses our system for latest sightings or star gazing opportunities.

I think from there it started slowly over months, very amateurish at first. We went from immersive experiences to Kennedy Space Center visits, rocket launch and staring at Hubble Telescope's lates images. An abundant fodder of fascinating discovery and development especially these past few years. Yet the mystery just as confounding in the face of the latest technology, spurring our curiosity. It seems the more we discover the leass we know.

On July 14th Kennnedy Space Center launched it's 25th mission to supply the International Space Ship - a joint effort between NASA and SpaceX. A collective cheer, filled the air as star struck gazers like us watched a giant ball of flame rise from the West and shoot across Cocoa beach in Florida. As the rocket broke away a frothing spiral of white clouds swirled down into giant loops until it fizzled away into dark black smoke. You could smell the smoke from the skies.



Artemis’s will soon be launching from the Kennedy Space Center, on August 29 2022. NASA is working with SpaceX to build the landing system to take astronauts from the lunar orbit to the moon surface.
