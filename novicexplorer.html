Novice Explorers

I recall my first conversation with my daughter. A random afternoon she looked up from her phone, ‘Mom I really want to be an Astro Biologist.”

“What is an Astro Biologist?”, I asked. “Oh it’s the study of life form on other planets and space,” she said matter of fact. I was intrigued by the thought and of her specific interest and the to come up with this and the research she’d done to see where she could learn more about the subject. We already trade stories and articles an area that sparked my own interest.

I think from there it started slow, very amateurish at first. We went from immersive experiences to Kennedy Space Center visits, star gazing. It’s been a very interesting two years for anyone following, The endless fodder of fascinating discovery and mystery on the peripheral of latest advancements and technology spurring our curiosity.

On July 14th Kenny Space Center launched the 25th re supply to the International Space, a joint effort between NASA and SpaceX. A collective cheer, filled the air as star struck gazers like us watched a giant ball of flame on the West shoot over Cocoa beach in Florida. It eventually formed a frothing spiral of white clouds until it fizzled into dark black smoke.
